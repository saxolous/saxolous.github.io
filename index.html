La San Dun Gita was written by Miguel Angel Lous, composer and saxophone player of the Miguel Angel Lous Jazz Group. The band currently consists of four other members including his partner Jana DeBusk, who provides the vocals. The members of the band have changed over time based on Miguel's whereabouts, however, they are currently active at Conservatory Maastricht in The Netherlands.

The Latin-Jazz band plays a variety of songs inspired by Amazon culture. La San Dun Gita is no exception and contains an interesting cultural story. This interactive website will not only explain how the song was written, but will also give a better insight into Miguel's ideas and inspiration.






The song 'La San Dun Gita' tells the story of an ancient Amazon ritual which aims to connect humans with their spiritual Nature guide. By combining the vines of Yage (Banisteriopsis Caapi) and Changru Panga (Psychotria viridis), a special mixture called Ayahuasca is brewed.

In the indigenous Amazon cultures, these two plants represent a male and female spirit. Only when combined together do they provide healing power. Ayahuasca is used for spiritual enlightenment as well as medicinal purposes in these practices.
